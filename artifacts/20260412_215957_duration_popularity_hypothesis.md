
## Hypothesis: Song Duration and Chart Popularity

**Direct Answer:** No, shorter songs are generally not more popular. Medium to longer duration songs tend to achieve higher popularity on the global chart.

### Hypothesis Statement:
Popularity on the global chart appears to be positively correlated with track duration, particularly favoring medium-length tracks. Tracks between **180s** and **240s** (medium duration) are the most popular, with an average rank of **46.05** across **57** tracks. This is significantly more popular than short tracks (under **180s**), which have an average rank of **59.66** across **32** tracks. Furthermore, the top **10%** most popular tracks are, on average, **20.6s** longer (**206.4s**) than the bottom **10%** least popular tracks (**185.8s**), reinforcing a trend where longer songs generally achieve higher popularity (indicated by a moderate negative Pearson correlation of **r = -0.2023** between duration and rank).

**Confidence:** Medium

**Confidence Reasoning:** The findings consistently point towards medium to longer tracks being more popular based on average rank and a moderate negative correlation between duration and rank. However, the correlation is not extremely strong, suggesting other factors are also at play.

### Supporting Evidence:
*   **Medium-duration tracks (180s-240s) are significantly more popular than short tracks (under 180s).**
    *   **Data:** Medium tracks: average rank **46.05** (**57** tracks); Short tracks: average rank **59.66** (**32** tracks).
*   **The top 10% most popular tracks are longer on average than the bottom 10% least popular tracks.**
    *   **Data:** Top **10%** average duration: **206.4s**; Bottom **10%** average duration: **185.8s**; a difference of **20.6s**.
*   **There is a moderate negative correlation between track duration and rank, implying longer songs tend to have lower (better) ranks.**
    *   **Data:** Pearson correlation **r = -0.2023**.

### Alternative Explanations:
1.  The observed correlation might be influenced by specific genres that naturally feature longer track lengths and are currently trending, rather than duration itself being a direct causal factor for popularity.
2.  The unusually low artist diversity on the chart, with only **4** unique artists across **100** tracks and one artist (**Morgan Wallen**) accounting for **4%** of tracks, could skew the duration-popularity relationship if these dominant artists consistently produce songs of a particular length.

### Summary:
Contrary to intuition, very short songs are generally not the most popular on the chart. Tracks of medium length, or even longer, tend to achieve higher chart positions, suggesting that more substantial song structures might resonate better with listeners currently.
